Mother Teresa

"A life not lived for others is not a life"

Early Life (1910-1928):

  • Mother Teresa, also known as Saint Teresa of Calcutta, was a renowned Catholic nun and humanitarian who dedicated her life to serving the poor and destitute.
  • Mother Teresa was born on August 26, 1910, in Skopje, which is now part of North Macedonia. Her birth name was Anjezë Gonxhe Bojaxhiu. She was born to Albanian parents and was the youngest of three children.
  • At the age of 12, she felt a calling to become a missionary and serve the poorest of the poor.

Becoming a Sister of Loreto (1928-1931):

  • In 1928, at the age of 18, Anjezë joined the Sisters of Loreto, an Irish Catholic order of nuns. She received her religious training in Dublin, Ireland, and then traveled to India to work as a teacher.

Founding the Missionaries of Charity (1946):

  • In 1946, Mother Teresa received what she described as a "call within a call." She felt a deep calling from God to leave the convent and work directly with the poor.
  • She received permission from the Vatican to leave her order and founded the Missionaries of Charity in Calcutta (now Kolkata), India. The mission was to care for the "poorest of the poor" and those in desperate need.

Work in the Slums (1950s-1990s):

  • Mother Teresa and her fellow sisters started their work in the slums of Calcutta, where they provided basic services such as food, medical care, and education to the impoverished residents.
  • Over the years, the Missionaries of Charity expanded their operations to include orphanages, hospices, and homes for people suffering from various ailments, including leprosy and HIV/AIDS.
  • Mother Teresa's work drew attention from around the world, and volunteers and donations poured in to support her efforts.

Nobel Peace Prize (1979):

  • In recognition of her extraordinary humanitarian work, Mother Teresa was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1979. The Nobel Committee praised her for her efforts to combat poverty and suffering.

Later Life and Canonization (1990s-2016):

  • Mother Teresa continued her work in Calcutta until declining health forced her to step down as the head of the Missionaries of Charity in 1997.
  • She passed away on September 5, 1997, at the age of 87.
  • Mother Teresa was beatified by the Catholic Church in 2003, a significant step toward sainthood.
  • She was canonized as a saint by Pope Francis on September 4, 2016, in recognition of her lifelong devotion to serving the poor and her inspirational example of selfless love and compassion.
